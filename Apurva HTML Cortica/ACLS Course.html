ACLS Course

The ACLS (Advanced Cardiovascular Life Support) course teaches healthcare professionals to manage life-threatening cardiovascular emergencies like cardiac arrest and stroke. It covers advanced airway management, defibrillation, and pharmacological interventions.

ACLS Course is designed to provide healthcare providers with the advanced skills and knowledge needed to manage patients experiencing life-threatening cardiovascular emergencies. This course focuses on improving outcomes through effective team dynamics and appropriate pharmacological and defibrillation techniques.

COURSE CONTENT/TOPICS COVERED

  • Normal Heart Anatomy and Physiology
  • The ACLS Survey (A-B-C-D)
  • Airway Management
  • Basic Airway Adjuncts
  • Basic Airway Technique
  • Advanced Airway Adjuncts
  • Routes Of Access
  • Pharmacological Tools
  • Principles Of Early Defibrillation
  • Key To Using An Automated External Defibrillator (AED)

Certification Period (Worldwide Recognition): 2 Years

Course Length: 4 – 5 Hours
