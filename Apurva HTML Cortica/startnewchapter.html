Start a New Chapter for CPR Healthcare

General Information

CPR Healthcare local chapters exist to support and promote CPR Healthcare and are incorporated under the CPR Healthcare Chapter Association (CHCA). They are designed to:

  • Support the national membership by providing educational and networking opportunities on a local level (provide CEUs).
  • Promote CPR Healthcare by expanding the organizationâ€™s visibility and membership.
  • Increase and promote national recognition and awareness of the business side of healthcare.
  • Provide a forum through which to administer CPR Healthcare certification examinations.
  • Provide personal growth through leadership opportunities as local chapter officers.

Criteria for Starting a New Local Chapter

All new local chapters are placed on a six-month probation period. The purpose of local chapters is to support CPR Healthcare members and act as an extension of CPR Healthcare. Requirements include:

  • Hold a minimum of six meetings per year where CEUs are offered.
  • Proctor a minimum of four exams per year.
  • Local chapters must not be used for personal promotion.

Chapter Officers

The member making the request must:

  • Be CPR Healthcare certified.
  • Be in good standing with CPR Healthcare.
  • Be willing to hold a leadership position in the proposed chapter.

Additional Information

For more details on local chapters and officer positions, refer to the CPR Healthcare Local Chapter Handbook. If you have read the requirements and agree to follow them, please complete the Start a New Chapter Application.
