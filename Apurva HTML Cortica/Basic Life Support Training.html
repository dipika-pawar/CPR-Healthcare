Basic Life Support Training

Basic Life Support (BLS) Training is a certification program that teaches individuals how to respond to life-threatening emergencies, such as cardiac arrest, respiratory distress, or choking. It covers essential skills like performing CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation), using an AED (Automated External Defibrillator), and providing emergency care until professional medical help arrives.

Basic Life Support (BLS) Training is designed to help students develop the knowledge, skills, and confidence to respond to a medical emergency. This 3-4 hour class is an excellent choice for healthcare providers and conforms to the 2015 Guidelines Update for CPR, ECC, and First Aid.

COURSE CONTENT/TOPICS COVERED

  • Key changes in basic life support, reflecting the new science from the 2015 American Heart Association Guidelines for Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation and Emergency Cardiovascular Care.
  • Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (CPR)
  • The American Heart Association Chain of Survival
  • 1 & 2 Rescuer CPR and AED for adult, child, and infant
  • Bag-mask techniques for adult, child, and infant
  • Rescue breathing for adult, child, and infant
  • Choking for adult, child, and infant
  • CPR with an advanced airway
  • How to check oxygen in cylinder
  • Oxygen carriage in body

Certification Period (Worldwide Recognition): 2 Years
